Class Mission

Your Mars habitat has a fixed total area of 1000 square kilometers. Use community needs to make your own decisions about zones, scale, and resource allocation.

You decide the functional zones. There is no single correct answer.

Student Level

Level 2: Complete the scale, zones, and area records independently, then explain your resource allocation choices.

    Before You Design

    Mars Habitat Design Platform Tutorial

    1. Understand the mission first

    The total area is fixed at 1000 square kilometers. The platform provides tools and record boxes, but it will not calculate area, ratios, or scale for you.

    2. Set the scale

    Open Math Records and enter “1 : ____” or “On the map, 1 cm = ____ km in real life.” The canvas grid is fixed at 40 × 25, and each square is 1 cm × 1 cm on the map.

    3. Place facilities

    Drag facilities from the 8-category asset library on the left, or click an asset and then click the canvas to place it. After placement, every facility can be selected, moved, rotated, resized, and renamed.

    4. Draw functional zones

    Click Zone in the bottom toolbar, then drag on the canvas to create an area. You decide the location and size. After selecting a zone, drag the corner handles to resize it.

    5. Manually enter area

    Click a facility or zone to open the edit panel. Enter its actual area, social function, and design notes yourself.

    6. Refer to population needs

    Review the 8 resident cards and consider childcare, worship, accessibility, multilingual education, mental health, greenery, and other needs.

    7. Choose a student level

    Level 1 focuses on basic records. Level 2 supports a complete independent design. Level 3 asks for deeper explanations about fairness, resource allocation, and community values.

    8. Present and export

    Enter presentation mode when you are ready to share. Export creates a design map and project report using only the content you entered.
